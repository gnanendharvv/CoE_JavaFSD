🚀 SpaceX's Latest Mission

SpaceX continues to make significant strides in space exploration with its latest mission, showcasing advanced technology and bringing us closer to interplanetary travel.

🌍 Mission Overview

On February 19, 2025, SpaceX successfully launched its **Starship spacecraft** atop the **Super Heavy booster** from Starbase, Texas. This mission, named **Starship Flight Test 7**, tested crucial capabilities like **orbit achievement, in-space engine relight, and re-entry procedures**.

🚀 Launch and Ascent

The launch took place at **4:00 PM CST**, with the Super Heavy booster providing the initial thrust. About **two minutes and thirty-nine seconds** into the flight, Starship separated from the booster. The booster then attempted a controlled landing in the Gulf of Mexico.

🛰️ Orbital Operations

Once in space, the Starship reached an **apogee of 212 km**. A major highlight of this mission was the **first-ever in-space relight of the Raptor engine**, a key step toward enabling longer space missions.

🌎 Re-entry and Splashdown

After spending nearly **47 minutes in orbit**, the spacecraft initiated its re-entry sequence. It performed a controlled descent and a **landing flip maneuver** before successfully splashing down in the **Indian Ocean**.

🚀 Why This Mission is Important

  • 📌 Demonstrates SpaceX’s progress toward deep-space missions.
  • 📌 Validates the **in-space relight** capability of Raptor engines.
  • 📌 Sets the stage for future **lunar and Mars missions**.
